## A-Tier: High Performance and Reliability

Units in the A-Tier are excellent for high-level play. While they may lack the "superpowers" of the S-Tier, their damage multipliers (typically between 160% and 185%) and reliable hitboxes make them staples for any serious player.

### The Power of Range
A common theme in A-Tier is the ability to hit enemies from all angles. The **Grius Medus**, for instance, is highly valued because it hits behind the character, making it perfect for leveling up while moving through dense mobs. Similarly, the **PP Kiwi** offers a 170% strength boost with an explosion that covers a wide radius, though its rarity as the second-rarest weather event keeps it out of S-Tier.

### Seasonal Standouts

## B-Tier: Solid but Flawed

B-Tier secrets are generally "good" but often have one major weakness—usually range or inconsistent hitboxes. The **Bananaito**, one of the original secrets, falls here because despite its 225% strength, it cannot hit anything beside or behind it.

*   **Trollo Quad:** Extremely wide but lacks special effects.
*   **Chickladder Biscyclera:** Incredible speed and easy to get, but low damage.
*   **Dragon Noni Canaloney:** Looks amazing, but the fire breath is notoriously difficult to aim.
*   **Nightmare Yoni:** A decent fusion, but the reach doesn't justify the cost of the three secrets required to craft it.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when trading for the **Lost Lolitos**. While they boast a 300% damage boost, their hitboxes are incredibly inconsistent. You may find yourself standing inside a boss and still missing your attacks.

## How to Acquire Secrets in 2026

To climb the ranks of the **brainrot evolution secrets tier list**, you need to know where to farm. Most secrets are obtained through specific weather conditions or dungeon drops.

1.  **Dungeon Crawling:** Units like the **Elbaante** and **Pot Hot Spot** are found exclusively in dungeons. These are great for solo players who don't want to rely on RNG weather.
2.  **Weather Hunting:** The rarest secrets, like the **PP Kiwi** or **Dragon Noni**, require you to wait for specific weather events. These have low spawn rates (often 1 in 45 or higher).
3.  **Fusion & Trading:** Visit the Kadabra Restaurant to combine lower-tier secrets into powerhouses like the **Strawberry Octoini**.
4.  **Admin Events:** Keep an eye out for developer-hosted events to get crates for units like the **I2 Perfect Genie**.

A: Generally, no. The **Puloopin** features a superior "tornado slash" animation with better area-of-effect (AoE) coverage. Most veteran players recommend keeping the Puloopin unless you specifically need the stat sheet of the Job Job for a very niche build.
